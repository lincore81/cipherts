In this game, your task is to solve substitution ciphers. Those are secret messages in which each letter is replaced by another, like all A's with X's, all X's with P's and so on.

To play, type a letter occuring in the cipher (notice that on the left the letter starts blinking), followed by either:

  • the letter you think it stands for,
  • space, which clears an existing binding or
  • escape to cancel.
If your device does not have a hardware keyboard, you can also click on any letter and then select a substitute from a list. At the moment this is not very convenient for mobile users, sorry for that. Repeat this process until you decoded the entire message.

You can use the find input-field to highlight character sequences in the cipher (alt+n in many browsers). The underscore "_" serves as wildcard for any one letter. For instance, you can highlight all three letter words with X at the end using " __X " (only if the cipher contains whitespace).

A few tips for the desperate:

  • E is the most commonly used letter in the English language
  • At first, try to decode short, very common words
  • Look for double letters
  • Look for common digraphs like TH, HE, IN, ER, AN etc.
  • If you are having significant problems on the lower difficulties, you may want to read up on frequency analysis.

cipherts, written by lincore in December 2015. Programmed in TypeScript (statically typed js with extras) with jQuery.

This is a recreation of the cryptography minigame in Sid Meier's Covert Action, developed by Microprose and released in 1990. I think it's a great game for its time. If you are interested, check it out on gog.com, steam or retroism.com (the current owner afaik).
